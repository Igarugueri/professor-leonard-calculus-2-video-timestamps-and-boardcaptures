в—Ҹ [0:00]. Introduction to first-order differential equations.
     в—ү Differential equations have broad applications, especially in engineering and applied sciences.
     в—ү Focus of the lecture:
          в—Ӣ separable differential equations
               в–  the most basic class of first-order differential equations

в—Ҹ [1:24]. Definition of a differential equation and the concept of order.
     в—ү A differential equation is an equation involving a derivative and an unknown function.
     в—ү Definition of order:
          в—Ӣ the order of a differential equation is the highest derivative that appears in it.
     в—ү First-order differential equations.
          в—Ӣ A first-order differential equation contains only a first derivative.

в—Ҹ [3:05]. Basic first-order equations of the form рқ’…рқ’ҡ/рқ’…рқ’ҷ = рқ’Ү(рқ’ҷ).
     в—ү [3:58]. Basic idea:
          в—Ӣ A first-order differential equation contains the first derivative of an unknown function.
          в—Ӣ Solving it means finding the function whose derivative satisfies the equation.
               в–  In simple cases such as
                    в–Ј рқ’…рқ’ҡ/рқ’…рқ’ҷ = рқ’Ү(рқ’ҷ),
                    в–Ј we can recover рқ’ҡ by integrating:
                         в–ў рқ’ҡ = вҲ« рқ’Ү(рқ’ҷ) рқ’…рқ’ҷ
     в—ү [4:23]. Limitation of this basic method.
          в—Ӣ It works only when the right-hand side depends entirely on рқ’ҷ:рқ’Ү(рқ’ҷ).
          в—Ӣ It does not immediately solve equations of the form рқ’…рқ’ҡ/рқ’…рқ’ҷ = рқ’Ү(рқ’ҷ, рқ’ҡ).

в—Ҹ [5:23]. First-order equations with mixed variables.
     в—ү General form:
          в—Ӣ рқ’…рқ’ҡ/рқ’…рқ’ҷ = рқ’Ү(рқ’ҷ, рқ’ҡ)
     в—ү Meaning of a solution:
          в—Ӣ A solution is a function рқ’ҡ = рқ’ҡ(рқ’ҷ), defined on some interval, such that
               в–  when we differentiate рқ’ҡ(рқ’ҷ), its derivative matches the right-hand side of the differential equation.
          в—Ӣ In other words,
               в–  рқ’ҡ = рқ’ҡ(рқ’ҷ) is a solution if рқ’…рқ’ҡ/рқ’…рқ’ҷ = рқ’Ү(рқ’ҷ, рқ’ҡ(рқ’ҷ)).
     в—ү Important idea:
          в—Ӣ We treat рқ’ҡ as a function of рқ’ҷ.
          в—Ӣ Even if рқ’ҡ is not given explicitly, it is still understood to depend on рқ’ҷ, just as in implicit differentiation.

в—Ҹ Verifying whether a proposed function is a solution to a differential equation.
     в—ү Strategy:
          в—Ӣ Differentiate the proposed function.
          в—Ӣ Then compare the result with the right-hand side of the differential equation.
     в—ү Key idea:
          в—Ӣ A proposed function is a solution if, after differentiating it,
               в–  the derivative satisfies the differential equation identically.
          в—Ӣ In other words,
               в–  substituting the function into the equation must make both sides equal on the given interval.

в—Ҹ [8:13]. рҹ§© Example 1 вҖ” Verify that рқ’ҡ = рқ’ҷ + 1 + рқ’„рқ’ҶЛЈ is a solution to рқ’ҡ' = рқ’ҡ вҲ’ рқ’ҷ on (вҲ’вҲһ, вҲһ). [рҹ“·image](./img/Calculus 2 Lecture 8.1/[8-13]Example 1 вҖ” Verify that рқ’ҡ = рқ’ҷ + 1 + рқ’„рқ’ҶЛЈ is a solution to рқ’ҡ' = рқ’ҡ вҲ’ рқ’ҷ on (вҲ’вҲһ, вҲһ).png)
     в—ү Step 1: Differentiate the proposed function.
          в—Ӣ рқ’ҡ = рқ’ҷ + 1 + рқ’„рқ’ҶЛЈ
          в—Ӣ Therefore,
               в–  рқ’…рқ’ҡ/рқ’…рқ’ҷ = 1 + рқ’„рқ’ҶЛЈ  = рқ’ҡ' (1)
     в—ү Step 2: Compute the right-hand side of the differential equation.
          в—Ӣ The equation requires:
               в–  рқ’ҡ' = рқ’ҡ вҲ’ рқ’ҷ
          в—Ӣ Since рқ’ҡ = рқ’ҷ + 1 + рқ’„рқ’ҶЛЈ,
               в–  рқ’ҡ' = рқ’ҡ вҲ’ рқ’ҷ = (рқ’ҷ + 1 + рқ’„рқ’ҶЛЈ) вҲ’ рқ’ҷ = 1 + рқ’„рқ’ҶЛЈ (2)
     в—ү Step 3: Compare both expressions.
          в—Ӣ From differentiation:
               в–  рқ’ҡ' = 1 + рқ’„рқ’ҶЛЈ (1)
          в—Ӣ From substitution into рқ’ҡ вҲ’ рқ’ҷ:
               в–  рқ’ҡ вҲ’ рқ’ҷ = 1 + рқ’„рқ’ҶЛЈ (2)
          в—Ӣ Therefore,
               в–  рқ’ҡ' = рқ’ҡ вҲ’ рқ’ҷ
     в—ү Conclusion:
          в—Ӣ The proposed function is indeed a solution on (вҲ’вҲһ, вҲһ).
     в—ү NOTE:
          в—Ӣ The constant рқ’„ remains arbitrary here.
          в—Ӣ That means this expression represents a family of solutions, not just one single curve.

в—Ҹ [14:41]. General solutions and arbitrary constants. [рҹ“·image](./img/Calculus 2 Lecture 8.1/[14-41]General solutions and arbitrary constants.png)
     в—ү [15:16]. A first-order differential equation typically has a solution with one arbitrary constant рқ’„.
     в—ү A solution containing one arbitrary constant is called a general solution.
     в—ү Geometric interpretation:
          в—Ӣ the general solution represents a family of integral curves. 

в—Ҹ [16:30]. Particular solutions and initial values. [рҹ“·image](./img/Calculus 2 Lecture 8.1/[16-30]Particular solutions and initial values.png)
     в—ү For a particular solution,
          в—Ӣ an initial value is required.
     в—ү Standard initial-value form:
          в—Ӣ рқ’ҡ(рқ’ҷвӮҖ) = рқ’ҡвӮҖ
     в—ү Geometric meaning:
          в—Ӣ the initial value selects one specific curve from the family of solutions.
          в—Ӣ Equivalently,
               в–  it forces the solution curve to pass through the fixed point (рқ’ҷвӮҖ, рқ’ҡвӮҖ).
     в—ү NOTE 1:
          в—Ӣ the arbitrary constant is what distinguishes one curve from another within the same family.
          в—Ӣ once the initial value is imposed,
               в–  the constant is determined,
               в–  and the general solution becomes a particular solution.
     в—ү NOTE 2:
          в—Ӣ Two different initial points can determine the same constant only if both points lie on the same particular solution curve.

в—Ҹ [20:55]. рҹ§© Example 2 вҖ” :Initial value problem with рқ’ҡ(0) = 0. [рҹ“·image](./img/Calculus 2 Lecture 8.1/[20-55]Example 2 вҖ” Initial value problem with рқ’ҡ(0) = 0.png)
     в—ү Use the previously verified general solution onрҹ§©Example 1:
          в—Ӣ рқ’ҡ = рқ’ҷ + 1 + рқ’„рқ’ҶЛЈ
     в—ү Substitute the initial value.
          в—Ӣ 0 = 0 + 1 + рқ’„рқ’ҶвҒ°
          в—Ӣ 0 = 1 + рқ’„
          в—Ӣ рқ’„ = вҲ’1
     в—ү Particular solution:
          в—Ӣ рқ’ҡ = рқ’ҷ + 1 вҲ’ рқ’ҶЛЈ
     в—ү Interpretation:
          в—Ӣ the initial condition reduces the general solution to one specific curve.

в—Ҹ [рҹ“·image](./img/Calculus 2 Lecture 8.1/[26-21]Definition of a separable differential equation.png)

в—Ҹ [26:21]. Definition of a separable differential equation.  
     в—ү A separable differential equation is a first-order differential equation that can be written as a product of:
          в—Ӣ a function of рқ’ҷ
          в—Ӣ and a function of рқ’ҡ
     в—ү Structural idea:
          в—Ӣ one factor depends on one variable,
          в—Ӣ and the other factor depends on the second variable.
     в—ү Main consequence:
          в—Ӣ the variables can be separated onto opposite sides of the equation.

в—Ҹ [28:34]. Standard forms of separable equations.
     в—ү Product form:
          в—Ӣ рқ’…рқ’ҡ/рқ’…рқ’ҷ = рқ“°(рқ’ҷ)рқ’ү(рқ’ҡ)
     в—ү Equivalent differential form:
          в—Ӣ рқ“ң(рқ’ҷ) рқ’…рқ’ҷ + рқ“қ(рқ’ҡ) рқ’…рқ’ҡ = 0
     в—ү These two forms are equivalent ways of expressing the same separable structure.

в—Ҹ [30:10]. Classification рҹ§© examples: separable vs. non-separable. 
     в—ү A differential equation is separable when all рқ’ҷ-terms can be placed on one side and all рқ’ҡ-terms on the other.
     в—ү If рқ’ҷ and рқ’ҡ remain mixed in a way that cannot be separated,
          в—Ӣ the equation is non-separable.
     в—ү Separable examples:
          в—Ӣ (рқ’ҷВІ + 1) рқ’…рқ’ҷ + 1/рқ’ҡ рқ’…рқ’ҡ = 0
               в–  already written with the рқ’ҷ-part and the рқ’ҡ-part separated
          в—Ӣ рқ’…рқ’ҡ/рқ’…рқ’• = рқ’Ңрқ’ҡ
               в–  can be viewed as рқ“°(рқ’•)рқ’ү(рқ’ҡ)
               в–  with рқ’Ң = рқ“°(рқ’•)
               в–  and рқ’ҡ = рқ’ү(рқ’ҡ)
     в—ү Non-separable examples:
          в—Ӣ рқ’…рқ’ҡ/рқ’…рқ’ҷ = рқ’ҷрқ’ҡВІ + 2рқ’ҷВІ
               в–  the terms do not factor into one function of рқ’ҷ times one function of рқ’ҡ
               в–  Compare with:
                    в–Ј рқ’…рқ’ҡ/рқ’…рқ’ҷ = рқ’ҷВІрқ’ҡВІ + 2рқ’ҷВІ
                    в–Ј = рқ’ҷВІ(рқ’ҡВІ + 2)
                    в–Ј this one is separable, because it can be written as
                         в–ў рқ“°(рқ’ҷ)рқ’ү(рқ’ҡ)
                    в–Ј with
                         в–ў рқ“°(рқ’ҷ) = рқ’ҷВІ
                         в–ў рқ’ү(рқ’ҡ) = рқ’ҡВІ + 2  
          в—Ӣ (рқ’ҷ + рқ’ҡ) рқ’…рқ’ҷ + рқ’ҷрқ’ҡ рқ’…рқ’ҡ = 0
               в–  the variables remain mixed and cannot be cleanly separated




пј§пҪ…пҪҺпҪ…пҪ’пҪҒпҪҢ пҪҚпҪ…пҪ”пҪҲпҪҸпҪ„ пҪҶпҪҸпҪ’ пҪ“пҪҸпҪҢпҪ–пҪүпҪҺпҪҮ пҪӮпҪҷ пҪ“пҪ…пҪҗпҪҒпҪ’пҪҒпҪ”пҪүпҪҸпҪҺ пҪҸпҪҶ пҪ–пҪҒпҪ’пҪүпҪҒпҪӮпҪҢпҪ…пҪ“пјҺ


в—Ҹ [35:10]. General method for solving by separation of variables. [рҹ“·image](./img/Calculus 2 Lecture 8.1/[35-10]General method for solving by separation of variables.png)
     в—ү Start with a separable equation:
          в—Ӣ рқ’…рқ’ҡ/рқ’…рқ’ҷ = рқ“°(рқ’ҷ) В· рқ’ү(рқ’ҡ)
     в—ү Rearrangement:
          в—Ӣ Divide both sides by рқ’ү(рқ’ҡ):
               в–  1/рқ’ү(рқ’ҡ) вӢ… рқ’…рқ’ҡ/рқ’…рқ’ҷ = рқ“°(рқ’ҷ)
          в—Ӣ Then separate the variables:
               в–  1/рқ’ү(рқ’ҡ) рқ’…рқ’ҡ = рқ“°(рқ’ҷ) рқ’…рқ’ҷ
     в—ү Integrate both sides.
          в—Ӣ вҲ« 1/рқ’ү(рқ’ҡ) рқ’…рқ’ҡ = вҲ« рқ“°(рқ’ҷ) рқ’…рқ’ҷ
     в—ү General solution form:
          в—Ӣ рқ“—(рқ’ҡ) = рқ“–(рқ’ҷ) + рқ’„
     в—ү Interpretation of the notation:
          в—Ӣ рқ“—(рқ’ҡ) is the antiderivative of 1/рқ’ү(рқ’ҡ) with respect to рқ’ҡ
          в—Ӣ рқ“–(рқ’ҷ) is the antiderivative of рқ“°(рқ’ҷ) with respect to рқ’ҷ
     в—ү Heuristic idea:
          в—Ӣ The step
               в–  1/рқ’ү(рқ’ҡ) вӢ… рқ’…рқ’ҡ/рқ’…рқ’ҷ = рқ“°(рқ’ҷ)
          в—Ӣ is treated as if the рқ’…рқ’ҷ moves to the other side:
               в–  1/рқ’ү(рқ’ҡ) рқ’…рқ’ҡ = рқ“°(рқ’ҷ) рқ’…рқ’ҷ
          в—Ӣ This is the standard separation-of-variables manipulation.
     в—ү Main idea:
          в—Ӣ first separate all рқ’ҡ-terms from all рқ’ҷ-terms
          в—Ӣ then integrate each side with respect to its own variable

в—Ҹ [40:21]. рҹ§© Example 1 вҖ” Solve рқ’…рқ’ҡ/рқ’…рқ’ҷ = рқ’ҡ/рқ’ҷ [рҹ“·image-1](./img/Calculus 2 Lecture 8.1/[40-21]Example 1 вҖ” Solve рқ’…рқ’ҡвҲ•рқ’…рқ’ҷ = рқ’ҡвҲ•рқ’ҷ_1.png) [рҹ“·image-2](./img/Calculus 2 Lecture 8.1/[40-21]Example 1 вҖ” Solve рқ’…рқ’ҡвҲ•рқ’…рқ’ҷ = рқ’ҡвҲ•рқ’ҷ_2.png)
     в—ү Recognize it as separable.
          в—Ӣ Rewrite:
               в–  1/рқ’ҡ вӢ… рқ’…рқ’ҡ/рқ’…рқ’ҷ = 1/рқ’ҷ
     в—ү Separate and integrate.
          в—Ӣ вҲ« 1/рқ’ҡ вӢ… рқ’…рқ’ҡ = вҲ« 1/рқ’ҷ вӢ… рқ’…рқ’ҷ
     в—ү Logarithmic antiderivatives:
          в—Ӣ ln|рқ’ҡ| = ln|рқ’ҷ| + рқ’„вӮҒ
     в—ү [44:27]. Constant manipulation.
          в—Ӣ Rewrite the constant in logarithmic form so it can be combined with the other logarithm:
               в–  рқ’„вӮҒ = ln|e^{рқ’„вӮҒ}|
               в–  Let рқ’„вӮӮ = e^{рқ’„вӮҒ}
               в–  Then рқ’„вӮҒ = ln|рқ’„вӮӮ|
     в—ү Combine logarithms.
          в—Ӣ ln|рқ’ҡ| = ln|рқ’ҷ| + ln|рқ’„вӮӮ|
          в—Ӣ = ln|рқ’ҷ вӢ… рқ’„вӮӮ|
     в—ү Exponentiate both sides.
          в—Ӣ e^{ln|рқ’ҡ|} = e^{ln|рқ’ҷ вӢ… рқ’„вӮӮ|}
          в—Ӣ so |рқ’ҡ| = |рқ’ҷ вӢ… рқ’„вӮӮ|
     в—ү Remove the absolute values.
          в—Ӣ рқ’ҡ = Вұрқ’ҷ вӢ… рқ’„вӮӮ
          в—Ӣ = Вұрқ’„вӮӮ рқ’ҷ
     в—ү Absorb the sign into the constant.
          в—Ӣ Let рқ’„ = Вұрқ’„вӮӮ
          в—Ӣ Therefore:
               в–  рқ’ҡ = рқ’„рқ’ҷ
     в—ү [50:40]. Final general solution.
          в—Ӣ рқ’ҡ = рқ’„рқ’ҷ
     в—ү NOTE:
          в—Ӣ In logarithmic separable equations, the constant is often rewritten and renamed so the logarithms can be combined cleanly.
          в—Ӣ The Вұ sign that appears after removing absolute values is absorbed into the arbitrary constant.

в—Ҹ [56:02]. рҹ§© Example 2 вҖ” Solve рқ’…рқ’ҡ/рқ’…рқ’ҷ = рқ’ҷрқ’ҡ/(рқ’ҷВІ + 1). [рҹ“·image-1](./img/Calculus 2 Lecture 8.1/[56-02]Example 2 вҖ” Solve рқ’…рқ’ҡвҲ•рқ’…рқ’ҷ = рқ’ҷрқ’ҡ вҲ• (рқ’ҷВІ + 1)_1.png) [рҹ“·image-2](./img/Calculus 2 Lecture 8.1/[56-02]Example 2 вҖ” Solve рқ’…рқ’ҡвҲ•рқ’…рқ’ҷ = рқ’ҷрқ’ҡ вҲ• (рқ’ҷВІ + 1)_2.png)
     в—ү Recognize it as separable.
          в—Ӣ Product form:
               в–  рқ’…рқ’ҡ/рқ’…рқ’ҷ = рқ“°(рқ’ҷ) вӢ… рқ’ү(рқ’ҡ)
               в–  рқ’…рқ’ҡ/рқ’…рқ’ҷ = (рқ’ҷ/(рқ’ҷВІ + 1)) вӢ… рқ’ҡ
     в—ү Separate the variables.
          в—Ӣ 1/рқ’ҡ вӢ… рқ’…рқ’ҡ/рқ’…рқ’ҷ = рқ’ҷ/(рқ’ҷВІ + 1)
          в—Ӣ hence
               в–  1/рқ’ҡ рқ’…рқ’ҡ = рқ’ҷ/(рқ’ҷВІ + 1) рқ’…рқ’ҷ
     в—ү Integrate both sides.
          в—Ӣ вҲ« 1/рқ’ҡ рқ’…рқ’ҡ = вҲ« рқ’ҷ/(рқ’ҷВІ + 1) рқ’…рқ’ҷ
          в—Ӣ ln|рқ’ҡ| = вҲ« рқ’ҷ/(рқ’ҷВІ + 1) рқ’…рқ’ҷ + рқ’„вӮҒ
     в—ү Use the substitution on the right-hand side.
          в—Ӣ Let рқ’– = рқ’ҷВІ + 1
          в—Ӣ then рқ’…рқ’– = 2рқ’ҷ рқ’…рқ’ҷ
          в—Ӣ so рқ’…рқ’–/2 = рқ’ҷ рқ’…рқ’ҷ
          в—Ӣ Therefore:
               в–  вҲ« рқ’ҷ/(рқ’ҷВІ + 1) рқ’…рқ’ҷ = 1/2 вҲ« 1/рқ’– рқ’…рқ’–
               в–  = 1/2 ln|рқ’–|
               в–  = 1/2 ln|рқ’ҷВІ + 1|
     в—ү So the integrated equation becomes:
          в—Ӣ ln|рқ’ҡ| = 1/2 ln|рқ’ҷВІ + 1| + рқ’„вӮҒ
     в—ү [1:02:15]. Rewrite the constant in logarithmic form.
          в—Ӣ рқ’„вӮҒ = ln|e^{рқ’„вӮҒ}|
          в—Ӣ Let рқ’„вӮӮ = e^{рқ’„вӮҒ}
          в—Ӣ Then:
               в–  рқ’„вӮҒ = ln|рқ’„вӮӮ|
     в—ү Substitute this back into the equation.
          в—Ӣ ln|рқ’ҡ| = 1/2 ln|рқ’ҷВІ + 1| + ln|рқ’„вӮӮ|
     в—ү Rewrite the factor 1/2 as an exponent.
          в—Ӣ 1/2 ln|рқ’ҷВІ + 1| = ln|(рқ’ҷВІ + 1)^{1/2}|
          в—Ӣ = ln|вҲҡ(рқ’ҷВІ + 1)|
     в—ү Combine the logarithms.
          в—Ӣ ln|рқ’ҡ| = ln|вҲҡ(рқ’ҷВІ + 1)| + ln|рқ’„вӮӮ|
          в—Ӣ = ln|вҲҡ(рқ’ҷВІ + 1) вӢ… рқ’„вӮӮ|
     в—ү Exponentiate both sides.
          в—Ӣ e^{ln|рқ’ҡ|} = e^{ln|вҲҡ(рқ’ҷВІ + 1) вӢ… рқ’„вӮӮ|}
          в—Ӣ so
               в–  |рқ’ҡ| = |вҲҡ(рқ’ҷВІ + 1) вӢ… рқ’„вӮӮ|
     в—ү Remove the absolute values.
          в—Ӣ рқ’ҡ = ВұвҲҡ(рқ’ҷВІ + 1) вӢ… рқ’„вӮӮ
          в—Ӣ = Вұрқ’„вӮӮ вҲҡ(рқ’ҷВІ + 1)
     в—ү Absorb the sign into the constant.
          в—Ӣ Let рқ’„ = Вұрқ’„вӮӮ
     в—ү  Final general solution.
          в—Ӣ рқ’ҡ = рқ’„вҲҡ(рқ’ҷВІ + 1)
     в—ү NOTE:
          в—Ӣ Since рқ’ҷВІ + 1 > 0 for every real рқ’ҷ,
               в–  вҲҡ(рқ’ҷВІ + 1) is always defined and positive.
          в—Ӣ As in the previous example,
               в–  the Вұ sign is absorbed into the arbitrary constant.





          

пјЎпҪ„пҪ–пҪҒпҪҺпҪғпҪ…пҪ„гҖҖпј©пҪҺпҪүпҪ”пҪүпҪҒпҪҢгҖҖпј¶пҪҒпҪҢпҪ•пҪ…гҖҖпј°пҪ’пҪҸпҪӮпҪҢпҪ…пҪҚ

в—Ҹ [1:09:33]. рҹ§© Example вҖ” Solve the IVP: (рқ’ҡВІ вҲ’ 1) рқ’…рқ’ҡ/рқ’…рқ’ҷ = вҲ’рқ’ҡрқ’ҶЛЈ with рқ’ҡ(0) = 1. [рҹ“·image](./img/Calculus 2 Lecture 8.1/[1-09-33]Example вҖ” Solve the IVP (рқ’ҡВІ вҲ’ 1) рқ’…рқ’ҡвҲ•рқ’…рқ’ҷ = вҲ’рқ’ҡрқ’ҶЛЈ with рқ’ҡ(0) = 1.png)
     в—ү Separate the variables.
          в—Ӣ (рқ’ҡВІ вҲ’ 1) рқ’…рқ’ҡ = вҲ’рқ’ҡрқ’ҶЛЈ рқ’…рқ’ҷ
          в—Ӣ (рқ’ҡВІ вҲ’ 1)/рқ’ҡ рқ’…рқ’ҡ = вҲ’рқ’ҶЛЈ рқ’…рқ’ҷ
     в—ү Simplify the рқ’ҡ-integrand.
          в—Ӣ (рқ’ҡВІ вҲ’ 1)/рқ’ҡ = рқ’ҡ вҲ’ 1/рқ’ҡ
     в—ү Integrate both sides.
          в—Ӣ вҲ« (рқ’ҡ вҲ’ 1/рқ’ҡ) рқ’…рқ’ҡ = вҲ« вҲ’рқ’ҶЛЈ рқ’…рқ’ҷ
          в—Ӣ 1/2 рқ’ҡВІ вҲ’ ln|рқ’ҡ| = вҲ’рқ’ҶЛЈ + рқ’„вӮҒ
     в—ү Multiply through by 2.
          в—Ӣ рқ’ҡВІ вҲ’ 2ln|рқ’ҡ| = вҲ’2рқ’ҶЛЈ + 2рқ’„вӮҒ
     в—ү Rewrite the logarithmic term.
          в—Ӣ Since 2ln|рқ’ҡ| = ln(рқ’ҡВІ),
               в–  рқ’ҡВІ вҲ’ ln(рқ’ҡВІ) = вҲ’2рқ’ҶЛЈ + рқ’„вӮӮ
               в–  where рқ’„вӮӮ = 2рқ’„вӮҒ
     в—ү Apply the initial value.
          в—Ӣ Substitute рқ’ҡ(0) = 1:
               в–  1ВІ вҲ’ ln(1ВІ) = вҲ’2рқ’ҶвҒ° + рқ’„вӮӮ
               в–  1 вҲ’ 0 = вҲ’2 + рқ’„вӮӮ
               в–  рқ’„вӮӮ = 3
     в—ү Particular implicit solution.
          в—Ӣ рқ’ҡВІ вҲ’ ln(рқ’ҡВІ) = вҲ’2рқ’ҶЛЈ + 3
     в—ү NOTE:
          в—Ӣ The solution is left in implicit form.
          в—Ӣ Solving explicitly for рқ’ҡ is not practical here.





          

пј®пҪҒпҪ”пҪ•пҪ’пҪҒпҪҢгҖҖпј§пҪ’пҪҸпҪ—пҪ”пҪҲгҖҖпјҶгҖҖпјӨпҪ…пҪғпҪҒпҪҷ

в—Ҹ [1:18:47]. Modeling natural growth and decay. [рҹ“·image](./img/Calculus 2 Lecture 8.1/[1-18-47]Modeling natural growth and decay.png)
     в—ү Basic idea:
          в—Ӣ Growth or decay occurs at a rate proportional to the size of the population.
     в—ү Important clarification:
          в—Ӣ рқ’ҡ = рқ’Ңрқ’• does not imply рқ’…рқ’ҡ/рқ’…рқ’• = рқ’Ңрқ’ҡ.
          в—Ӣ In fact, if рқ’ҡ = рқ’Ңрқ’•, then
               в–  рқ’…рқ’ҡ/рқ’…рқ’• = рқ’Ң
          в—Ӣ For natural growth and decay, the correct assumption is:
               в–  the rate of change is proportional to the current population
          в—Ӣ So:
               в–  рқ’…рқ’ҡ/рқ’…рқ’• вҲқ рқ’ҡ
               в–  hence рқ’…рқ’ҡ/рқ’…рқ’• = рқ’Ңрқ’ҡ
          в—Ӣ In words:
               в–  the rate of change of the population is proportional to the population itself.
     в—ү Differential equation model:
          в—Ӣ рқ’…рқ’ҡ/рқ’…рқ’• = рқ’Ңрқ’ҡ
          в—Ӣ where рқ’ҡ = рқ’ҡ(рқ’•)
     в—ү Interpretation of the derivative:
          в—Ӣ рқ’…рқ’ҡ/рқ’…рқ’• measures how fast the population changes with respect to time.
          в—Ӣ If рқ’…рқ’ҡ/рқ’…рқ’• > 0,
               в–  the population is increasing.
          в—Ӣ If рқ’…рқ’ҡ/рқ’…рқ’• < 0,
               в–  the population is decreasing.
     в—ү Interpretation of рқ’Ң:
          в—Ӣ if рқ’Ң > 0,
               в–  the population grows
          в—Ӣ if рқ’Ң < 0,
               в–  the population decays
     в—ү Initial value:
          в—Ӣ рқ’ҡ(0) = рқ’ҡвӮҖ
          в—Ӣ рқ’ҡвӮҖ represents the starting population.

в—Ҹ [1:23:26]. Derivation of the exponential growth/decay law. [рҹ“·image-1](./img/Calculus 2 Lecture 8.1/[1-23-26]Derivation of the exponential growth вҲ• decay law_1.png) [рҹ“·image-2](./img/Calculus 2 Lecture 8.1/[1-23-26]Derivation of the exponential growth вҲ• decay law_2.png)
     в—ү Start from the model:
          в—Ӣ рқ’…рқ’ҡ/рқ’…рқ’• = рқ’Ңрқ’ҡ
     в—ү Separate variables:
          в—Ӣ 1/рқ’ҡ вӢ… рқ’…рқ’ҡ/рқ’…рқ’• = рқ’Ң
          в—Ӣ hence
               в–  1/рқ’ҡ рқ’…рқ’ҡ = рқ’Ң рқ’…рқ’•
     в—ү Integrate both sides:
          в—Ӣ вҲ« 1/рқ’ҡ рқ’…рқ’ҡ = вҲ« рқ’Ң рқ’…рқ’•
          в—Ӣ ln|рқ’ҡ| = рқ’Ңрқ’• + рқ’„вӮҒ
     в—ү Exponentiate:
          в—Ӣ e^{ln|рқ’ҡ|} = e^{рқ’Ңрқ’• + рқ’„вӮҒ}
          в—Ӣ |рқ’ҡ| = e^{рқ’Ңрқ’•} вӢ… e^{рқ’„вӮҒ}
     в—ү Rewrite the constant:
          в—Ӣ Let рқ’„вӮӮ = e^{рқ’„вӮҒ}
          в—Ӣ then
               в–  |рқ’ҡ| = рқ’„вӮӮ e^{рқ’Ңрқ’•}
     в—ү Remove the absolute value:
          в—Ӣ рқ’ҡ = Вұрқ’„вӮӮ e^{рқ’Ңрқ’•}
     в—ү Absorb the sign into the constant:
          в—Ӣ Let рқ’„ = Вұрқ’„вӮӮ
          в—Ӣ therefore
               в–  рқ’ҡ = рқ’„e^{рқ’Ңрқ’•}
     в—ү Use the initial value рқ’ҡ(0) = рқ’ҡвӮҖ:
          в—Ӣ рқ’ҡвӮҖ = рқ’„eвҒ°
          в—Ӣ рқ’ҡвӮҖ = рқ’„
     в—ү Final model:
          в—Ӣ рқ’ҡ = рқ’ҡвӮҖe^{рқ’Ңрқ’•}
     в—ү Interpretation:
          в—Ӣ рқ’ҡвӮҖ is the initial population.
          в—Ӣ The constant рқ’Ң determines whether the model describes growth or decay.




          

пјӯпҪҸпҪ„пҪ…пҪҢгҖҖпјЎпҪ“пҪ“пҪ•пҪҚпҪҗпҪ”пҪүпҪҸпҪҺпҪ“гҖҖпјҰпҪҸпҪ’гҖҖпј®пҪҒпҪ”пҪ•пҪ’пҪҒпҪҢгҖҖпј§пҪ’пҪҸпҪ—пҪ”пҪҲгҖҖпјҶгҖҖпјӨпҪ…пҪғпҪҒпҪҷ

в—Ҹ [1:44:15]. Modeling assumption for the exponential growth/decay model.
     в—ү Core assumption:
          в—Ӣ The equation рқ’…рқ’ҡ/рқ’…рқ’• = рқ’Ңрқ’ҡ can only be used when the problem states that the growth or decay rate is **proportional** to the current population.
          в—Ӣ This proportionality cannot be assumed unless it is explicitly given in the statement.
     в—ү Important remark:
          в—Ӣ Not every growth or decay process is proportional to the population size.
          в—Ӣ Some external effects may act non-proportionally, so the model рқ’…рқ’ҡ/рқ’…рқ’• = рқ’Ңрқ’ҡ would no longer be appropriate.
     в—ү Example of a non-proportional effect:
          в—Ӣ If an external agent acts independently of the current population size,
               в–  the population may decrease in a way that is not proportional to рқ’ҡ.
          в—Ӣ In such a case,
               в–  the exponential model should not be assumed.

1 - пјўпҪҒпҪғпҪ”пҪ…пҪ’пҪүпҪҒпҪҢгҖҖпј§пҪ’пҪҸпҪ—пҪ”пҪҲ

в—Ҹ [1:46:45]. рҹ§© Application вҖ” Bacterial growth. [рҹ“·image](./img/Calculus 2 Lecture 8.1/[1-46-45]Application вҖ” Bacterial growth.png)
     в—ү Growth of bacteria is proportional to its population. Initially, 600 bacteria are present. After 3 hours, 10,000 bacteria are present.
     в—ү Modeling assumption:
          в—Ӣ the growth of the bacteria is proportional to its population.
     в—ү Initial data:
          в—Ӣ initially there are 600 bacteria
          в—Ӣ after 3 hours there are 10,000 bacteria
     в—ү Goal:
          в—Ӣ find the number of bacteria after рқ’• hours.
     в—ү Start from the natural growth model:
          в—Ӣ рқ’ҡ = рқ’ҡвӮҖe^{рқ’Ңрқ’•}
     в—ү Substitute the initial population.
          в—Ӣ рқ’ҡ = 600e^{рқ’Ңрқ’•}
     в—ү Use the second data point:
          в—Ӣ рқ’ҡ(3) = 10{,}000
          в—Ӣ 10{,}000 = 600e^{3рқ’Ң}
          в—Ӣ 50/3 = e^{3рқ’Ң}
     в—ү Solve more efficiently for e^рқ’Ң.
          в—Ӣ (50/3)^{1/3} = (e^{3рқ’Ң})^{1/3}
          в—Ӣ e^рқ’Ң = (50/3)^{1/3}
     в—ү Rewrite the model.
          в—Ӣ рқ’ҡ = 600e^{рқ’Ңрқ’•}
          в—Ӣ = 600(e^рқ’Ң)^рқ’•
          в—Ӣ = 600[(50/3)^{1/3}]^рқ’•

     в—ү Final model:
          в—Ӣ рқ’ҡ = 600(50/3)^{рқ’•/3}

     в—ү Substitute рқ’• = 5 into the model.

     в—ү Numerical value:
          в—Ӣ рқ’ҡ вүҲ 65248 bacteria

в—Ҹ [1:58:13]. Time required to reach 24,000 bacteria.
     в—ү Set

     в—ү Simplify:

     в—ү Take logarithms:

     в—ү Solve for рқ’•:

     в—ү Approximate time:

в—Ҹ [2:02:35]. рҹ§© Application вҖ” Half-life and radioactive decay model. [рҹ“·image-1](./img/Calculus 2 Lecture 8.1/[2-02-35]Application вҖ” Half-life and radioactive decay model_1.png) [рҹ“·image-2](./img/Calculus 2 Lecture 8.1/[2-02-35]Application вҖ” Half-life and radioactive decay model_2.png)
     в—ү Suppose the half-life of compound A is 1602 years. We have 100 mg to start. Find the amount after рқ’• years.
     в—ү Given:

          в—Ӣ initial amount = 100 mg
     в—ү Goal:
          в—Ӣ find the amount after рқ’• years.
     в—ү Start from the natural decay model:
          в—Ӣ рқ’ҡ = рқ’ҡвӮҖe^{рқ’Ңрқ’•}
     в—ү Substitute the initial amount.
          в—Ӣ рқ’ҡ = 100e^{рқ’Ңрқ’•}
     в—ү Half-life condition:

               в–  рқ’ҡ(1602) = 50

     в—ү Differential equation model:

               в–  but on how far рқ’ҡ is from the ambient temperature.

3.1 - пјҙпҪ•пҪ’пҪӢпҪ…пҪҷгҖҖпјЈпҪҸпҪҸпҪҢпҪүпҪҺпҪҮгҖҖпјЈпҪҒпҪ“пҪ…гҖҖпјіпҪ”пҪ•пҪ„пҪҷ

в—Ҹ [рҹ“·image-1](./img/Calculus 2 Lecture 8.1/[2-22-22]Case study вҖ” Cooling of a turkey_1.png) [рҹ“·image-2](./img/Calculus 2 Lecture 8.1/[2-22-22]Case study вҖ” Cooling of a turkey_2.png) [рҹ“·image-3](./img/Calculus 2 Lecture 8.1/[2-22-22]Case study вҖ” Cooling of a turkey_3.png) [рҹ“·image-4](./img/Calculus 2 Lecture 8.1/[2-22-22]Case study вҖ” Cooling of a turkey_4.png) 

в—Ҹ [2:22:22]. рҹ§© Case study вҖ” Cooling of a turkey.
     в—ү A turkey is taken out of an oven at 225В°. After 20 min, the temperature was 185В°. The temperature of my house was 70В°.
     в—ү Conditions:
          в—Ӣ ambient temperature рқ’ҡ_рқ’Ӯ = 70
          в—Ӣ рқ’ҡ(0) = 225
          в—Ӣ рқ’ҡ(20) = 185
     в—ү Interpretation of the variables:
          в—Ӣ рқ’ҡ(рқ’•) = temperature of the turkey
          в—Ӣ рқ’• = time in minutes
     в—ү Substitute the ambient temperature into the model:
          в—Ӣ рқ’…рқ’ҡ/рқ’…рқ’• = рқ’Ң(рқ’ҡ вҲ’ 70)

          в—Ӣ |рқ’ҡ вҲ’ 70| = e^{рқ’Ңрқ’•} вӢ… e^{рқ’„вӮҒ}
     в—ү Rewrite the constant:

          в—Ӣ then

          в—Ӣ therefore
               в–  рқ’ҡ вҲ’ 70 = рқ’„e^{рқ’Ңрқ’•}
