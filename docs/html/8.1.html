  • 0:00. Introduction to first-order differential equations.
    • Differential equations have broad applications, especially in engineering and applied sciences.
    • Focus of the lecture:
      • separable differential equations
        • the most basic class of first-order differential equations
  • 1:24. Definition of a differential equation and the concept of order.
    • A differential equation is an equation involving a derivative and an unknown function.
    • Definition of order:
      • the order of a differential equation is the highest derivative that appears in it.
    • First-order differential equations.
      • A first-order differential equation contains only a first derivative.
  • 3:05. Basic first-order equations of the form $\dfrac{рқ’…рқ’ҡ}{рқ’…рқ’ҷ}=рқ’Ү(рқ’ҷ)$.
    • 3:58. Basic idea:
      • A first-order differential equation contains the first derivative of an unknown function.
      • Solving it means finding the function whose derivative satisfies the equation.
        • In simple cases such as
          • $\dfrac{рқ’…рқ’ҡ}{рқ’…рқ’ҷ}=рқ’Ү(рқ’ҷ)$,
          • we can recover $рқ’ҡ$ by integrating:
            • $рқ’ҡ=\displaystyle \int рқ’Ү(рқ’ҷ)\,рқ’…рқ’ҷ$
    • 4:23. Limitation of this basic method.
      • It works only when the right-hand side depends entirely on $рқ’ҷ$: $рқ’Ү(рқ’ҷ)$.
      • It does not immediately solve equations of the form $\dfrac{рқ’…рқ’ҡ}{рқ’…рқ’ҷ}=рқ’Ү(рқ’ҷ,рқ’ҡ)$.
  • 5:23. First-order equations with mixed variables.
    • General form:
      • $\dfrac{рқ’…рқ’ҡ}{рқ’…рқ’ҷ}=рқ’Ү(рқ’ҷ,рқ’ҡ)$
    • Meaning of a solution:
      • A solution is a function $рқ’ҡ=рқ’ҡ(рқ’ҷ)$, defined on some interval, such that
        • when we differentiate $рқ’ҡ(рқ’ҷ)$, its derivative matches the right-hand side of the differential equation.
      • In other words,
        • $рқ’ҡ=рқ’ҡ(рқ’ҷ)$ is a solution if $\dfrac{рқ’…рқ’ҡ}{рқ’…рқ’ҷ}=рқ’Ү(рқ’ҷ,рқ’ҡ(рқ’ҷ))$.
    • Important idea:
      • We treat $рқ’ҡ$ as a function of $рқ’ҷ$.
      • Even if $рқ’ҡ$ is not given explicitly, it is still understood to depend on $рқ’ҷ$, just as in implicit differentiation.

пј¶пҪ…пҪ’пҪүпҪҶпҪҷпҪүпҪҺпҪҮгҖҖпјіпҪҸпҪҢпҪ•пҪ”пҪүпҪҸпҪҺпҪ“

  • Verifying whether a proposed function is a solution to a differential equation.
    • Strategy:
      • Differentiate the proposed function.
      • Then compare the result with the right-hand side of the differential equation.
    • Key idea:
      • A proposed function is a solution if, after differentiating it,
        • the derivative satisfies the differential equation identically.
      • In other words,
        • substituting the function into the equation must make both sides equal on the given interval.
  • 8:13. рҹ§© Example 1 вҖ” Verify that $рқ’ҡ=рқ’ҷ+1+рқ’„рқ’Ҷ^{рқ’ҷ}$ is a solution to $рқ’ҡ'=рқ’ҡ-рқ’ҷ$ on $(-\infty,\infty)$. вҖ“ [рҹ“·image]
    • Step 1: Differentiate the proposed function.
      • $рқ’ҡ=рқ’ҷ+1+рқ’„рқ’Ҷ^{рқ’ҷ}$
      • Therefore,
        • $\dfrac{рқ’…рқ’ҡ}{рқ’…рқ’ҷ}=1+рқ’„рқ’Ҷ^{рқ’ҷ}=рқ’ҡ' \;\;(1)$
    • Step 2: Compute the right-hand side of the differential equation.
      • The equation requires:
        • $рқ’ҡ'=рқ’ҡ-рқ’ҷ$
      • Since $рқ’ҡ=рқ’ҷ+1+рқ’„рқ’Ҷ^{рқ’ҷ}$,
        • $рқ’ҡ-рқ’ҷ=(рқ’ҷ+1+рқ’„рқ’Ҷ^{рқ’ҷ})-рқ’ҷ=1+рқ’„рқ’Ҷ^{рқ’ҷ}\;\;(2)$
    • Step 3: Compare both expressions.
      • From differentiation:
        • $рқ’ҡ'=1+рқ’„рқ’Ҷ^{рқ’ҷ}\;\;(1)$
      • From substitution into $рқ’ҡ-рқ’ҷ$:
        • $рқ’ҡ-рқ’ҷ=1+рқ’„рқ’Ҷ^{рқ’ҷ}\;\;(2)$
      • Therefore,
        • $рқ’ҡ'=рқ’ҡ-рқ’ҷ$
    • Conclusion:
      • The proposed function is indeed a solution on $(-\infty,\infty)$.
    • NOTE:
      • The constant $рқ’„$ remains arbitrary here.
      • That means this expression represents a family of solutions, not just one single curve.

    • 15:16. A first-order differential equation typically has a solution with one arbitrary constant $рқ’„$.
    • A solution containing one arbitrary constant is called a general solution.
    • Geometric interpretation:
      • the general solution represents a family of integral curves.
  • 16:30. Particular solutions and initial values. вҖ“ [рҹ“·image]
    • For a particular solution,
      • an initial value is required.
    • Standard initial-value form:
      • $рқ’ҡ(рқ’ҷ_{0})=рқ’ҡ_{0}$
    • Geometric meaning:
      • the initial value selects one specific curve from the family of solutions.
      • Equivalently,
        • it forces the solution curve to pass through the fixed point $(рқ’ҷ_{0},рқ’ҡ_{0})$.
    • NOTE 1:
      • the arbitrary constant is what distinguishes one curve from another within the same family.
      • once the initial value is imposed,
        • the constant is determined,
        • and the general solution becomes a particular solution.
    • NOTE 2:
      • Two different initial points can determine the same constant only if both points lie on the same particular solution curve.

  • 26:21. Definition of a separable differential equation.
    • A separable differential equation is a first-order differential equation that can be written as a product of:
      • a function of $рқ’ҷ$
      • and a function of $рқ’ҡ$
    • Structural idea:
      • one factor depends on one variable,
      • and the other factor depends on the second variable.
    • Main consequence:
      • the variables can be separated onto opposite sides of the equation.
  • 28:34. Standard forms of separable equations.
    • Product form:
      • $\dfrac{рқ’…рқ’ҡ}{рқ’…рқ’ҷ}=рқ“°(рқ’ҷ)рқ’ү(рқ’ҡ)$
    • Equivalent differential form:
      • $\mathcal{M}(рқ’ҷ)\,рқ’…рқ’ҷ+\mathcal{N}(рқ’ҡ)\,рқ’…рқ’ҡ=0$
    • These two forms are equivalent ways of expressing the same separable structure.
  • 30:10. Classification рҹ§© examples: separable vs. non-separable.
    • A differential equation is separable when all $рқ’ҷ$-terms can be placed on one side and all $рқ’ҡ$-terms on the other.
    • If $рқ’ҷ$ and $рқ’ҡ$ remain mixed in a way that cannot be separated,
      • the equation is non-separable.
    • Separable examples:
      • $(рқ’ҷ^{2}+1)\,рқ’…рқ’ҷ+\dfrac{1}{рқ’ҡ}\,рқ’…рқ’ҡ=0$
        • already written with the $рқ’ҷ$-part and the $рқ’ҡ$-part separated
      • $\dfrac{рқ’…рқ’ҡ}{рқ’…рқ’•}=рқ’Ңрқ’ҡ$
        • can be viewed as $рқ“°(рқ’•)рқ’ү(рқ’ҡ)$
        • with $рқ’Ң=рқ“°(рқ’•)$
        • and $рқ’ҡ=рқ’ү(рқ’ҡ)$
    • Non-separable examples:
      • $\dfrac{рқ’…рқ’ҡ}{рқ’…рқ’ҷ}=рқ’ҷрқ’ҡ^{2}+2рқ’ҷ^{2}$
        • the terms do not factor into one function of $рқ’ҷ$ times one function of $рқ’ҡ$
        • Compare with:
          • $\dfrac{рқ’…рқ’ҡ}{рқ’…рқ’ҷ}=рқ’ҷ^{2}рқ’ҡ^{2}+2рқ’ҷ^{2}$
          • $=рқ’ҷ^{2}(рқ’ҡ^{2}+2)$
          • this one is separable, because it can be written as
            • $рқ“°(рқ’ҷ)рқ’ү(рқ’ҡ)$
          • with
            • $рқ“°(рқ’ҷ)=рқ’ҷ^{2}$
            • $рқ’ү(рқ’ҡ)=рқ’ҡ^{2}+2$
      • $(рқ’ҷ+рқ’ҡ)\,рқ’…рқ’ҷ+рқ’ҷрқ’ҡ\,рқ’…рқ’ҡ=0$
        • the variables remain mixed and cannot be cleanly separated

  • 35:10. General method for solving by separation of variables. вҖ“ [рҹ“·image]
    • Start with a separable equation:
      • $\dfrac{рқ’…рқ’ҡ}{рқ’…рқ’ҷ}=рқ“°(рқ’ҷ)\cdot рқ’ү(рқ’ҡ)$
    • Rearrangement:
      • Divide both sides by $рқ’ү(рқ’ҡ)$:
        • $\dfrac{1}{рқ’ү(рқ’ҡ)}\cdot \dfrac{рқ’…рқ’ҡ}{рқ’…рқ’ҷ}=рқ“°(рқ’ҷ)$
      • Then separate the variables:
        • $\dfrac{1}{рқ’ү(рқ’ҡ)}\,рқ’…рқ’ҡ=рқ“°(рқ’ҷ)\,рқ’…рқ’ҷ$
    • Integrate both sides.
      • $\displaystyle \int \dfrac{1}{рқ’ү(рқ’ҡ)}\,рқ’…рқ’ҡ=\int рқ“°(рқ’ҷ)\,рқ’…рқ’ҷ$
    • General solution form:
      • $\mathcal{H}(рқ’ҡ)=\mathcal{G}(рқ’ҷ)+рқ’„$
    • Interpretation of the notation:
      • $\mathcal{H}(рқ’ҡ)$ is the antiderivative of $\dfrac{1}{рқ’ү(рқ’ҡ)}$ with respect to $рқ’ҡ$
      • $\mathcal{G}(рқ’ҷ)$ is the antiderivative of $рқ“°(рқ’ҷ)$ with respect to $рқ’ҷ$
    • Heuristic idea:
      • The step
        • $\dfrac{1}{рқ’ү(рқ’ҡ)}\cdot \dfrac{рқ’…рқ’ҡ}{рқ’…рқ’ҷ}=рқ“°(рқ’ҷ)$
      • is treated as if the $рқ’…рқ’ҷ$ moves to the other side:
        • $\dfrac{1}{рқ’ү(рқ’ҡ)}\,рқ’…рқ’ҡ=рқ“°(рқ’ҷ)\,рқ’…рқ’ҷ$
      • This is the standard separation-of-variables manipulation.
    • Main idea:
      • first separate all $рқ’ҡ$-terms from all $рқ’ҷ$-terms
      • then integrate each side with respect to its own variable
  • 40:21. рҹ§© Example 1 вҖ” Solve $\dfrac{рқ’…рқ’ҡ}{рқ’…рқ’ҷ}=\dfrac{рқ’ҡ}{рқ’ҷ}$ вҖ“ [рҹ“·image-1] вҖ“ [рҹ“·image-2]
    • Recognize it as separable.
      • Rewrite:
        • $\dfrac{1}{рқ’ҡ}\cdot \dfrac{рқ’…рқ’ҡ}{рқ’…рқ’ҷ}=\dfrac{1}{рқ’ҷ}$
    • Separate and integrate.
      • $\displaystyle \int \dfrac{1}{рқ’ҡ}\,рқ’…рқ’ҡ=\int \dfrac{1}{рқ’ҷ}\,рқ’…рқ’ҷ$
    • Logarithmic antiderivatives:
      • $\ln|рқ’ҡ|=\ln|рқ’ҷ|+рқ’„_{1}$
    • 44:27. Constant manipulation.
      • Rewrite the constant in logarithmic form so it can be combined with the other logarithm:
        • $рқ’„_{1}=\ln|рқ’Ҷ^{рқ’„_{1}}|$
        • Let $рқ’„_{2}=рқ’Ҷ^{рқ’„_{1}}$
        • Then $рқ’„_{1}=\ln|рқ’„_{2}|$
    • Combine logarithms.
      • $\ln|рқ’ҡ|=\ln|рқ’ҷ|+\ln|рқ’„_{2}|$
      • $=\ln|рқ’ҷ\cdot рқ’„_{2}|$
    • Exponentiate both sides.
      • $рқ’Ҷ^{\ln|рқ’ҡ|}=рқ’Ҷ^{\ln|рқ’ҷ\cdot рқ’„_{2}|}$
      • so $|рқ’ҡ|=|рқ’ҷ\cdot рқ’„_{2}|$
    • Remove the absolute values.
      • $рқ’ҡ=\pm рқ’ҷ\cdot рқ’„_{2}$
      • $=\pm рқ’„_{2}\,рқ’ҷ$
    • Absorb the sign into the constant.
      • Let $рқ’„=\pm рқ’„_{2}$
      • Therefore:
        • $рқ’ҡ=рқ’„рқ’ҷ$
    • 50:40. Final general solution.
      • $рқ’ҡ=рқ’„рқ’ҷ$
    • NOTE:
      • In logarithmic separable equations, the constant is often rewritten and renamed so the logarithms can be combined cleanly.
      • The $\pm$ sign that appears after removing absolute values is absorbed into the arbitrary constant.
  • 56:02. рҹ§© Example 2 вҖ” Solve $\dfrac{рқ’…рқ’ҡ}{рқ’…рқ’ҷ}=\dfrac{рқ’ҷрқ’ҡ}{рқ’ҷ^{2}+1}$. вҖ“ [рҹ“·image-1] вҖ“ [рҹ“·image-2]
    • Recognize it as separable.

        • $\dfrac{рқ’…рқ’ҡ}{рқ’…рқ’ҷ}=\left(\dfrac{рқ’ҷ}{рқ’ҷ^{2}+1}\right)\cdot рқ’ҡ$
    • Separate the variables.
      • $\dfrac{1}{рқ’ҡ}\cdot \dfrac{рқ’…рқ’ҡ}{рқ’…рқ’ҷ}=\dfrac{рқ’ҷ}{рқ’ҷ^{2}+1}$
      • hence

    • Integrate both sides.
      • $\displaystyle \int \dfrac{1}{рқ’ҡ}\,рқ’…рқ’ҡ=\int \dfrac{рқ’ҷ}{рқ’ҷ^{2}+1}\,рқ’…рқ’ҷ$
      • $\ln|рқ’ҡ|=\displaystyle \int \dfrac{рқ’ҷ}{рқ’ҷ^{2}+1}\,рқ’…рқ’ҷ+рқ’„_{1}$
    • Use the substitution on the right-hand side.

      • then $рқ’…рқ’–=2рқ’ҷ\,рқ’…рқ’ҷ$
      • so $\dfrac{рқ’…рқ’–}{2}=рқ’ҷ\,рқ’…рқ’ҷ$

        • $=\dfrac{1}{2}\ln|рқ’–|$
        • $=\dfrac{1}{2}\ln|рқ’ҷ^{2}+1|$
    • So the integrated equation becomes:
      • $\ln|рқ’ҡ|=\dfrac{1}{2}\ln|рқ’ҷ^{2}+1|+рқ’„_{1}$
    • 1:02:15. Rewrite the constant in logarithmic form.
      • $рқ’„_{1}=\ln|рқ’Ҷ^{рқ’„_{1}}|$
      • Let $рқ’„_{2}=рқ’Ҷ^{рқ’„_{1}}$
      • Then:
        • $рқ’„_{1}=\ln|рқ’„_{2}|$
    • Substitute this back into the equation.
      • $\ln|рқ’ҡ|=\dfrac{1}{2}\ln|рқ’ҷ^{2}+1|+\ln|рқ’„_{2}|$
    • Rewrite the factor $\dfrac{1}{2}$ as an exponent.
      • $\dfrac{1}{2}\ln|рқ’ҷ^{2}+1|=\ln|(рқ’ҷ^{2}+1)^{1/2}|$
      • $=\ln|\sqrt{рқ’ҷ^{2}+1}|$
    • Combine the logarithms.
      • $\ln|рқ’ҡ|=\ln|\sqrt{рқ’ҷ^{2}+1}|+\ln|рқ’„_{2}|$
      • $=\ln|\sqrt{рқ’ҷ^{2}+1}\cdot рқ’„_{2}|$
    • Exponentiate both sides.
      • $рқ’Ҷ^{\ln|рқ’ҡ|}=рқ’Ҷ^{\ln|\sqrt{рқ’ҷ^{2}+1}\cdot рқ’„_{2}|}$
      • so
        • $|рқ’ҡ|=|\sqrt{рқ’ҷ^{2}+1}\cdot рқ’„_{2}|$
    • Remove the absolute values.
      • $рқ’ҡ=\pm \sqrt{рқ’ҷ^{2}+1}\cdot рқ’„_{2}$
      • $=\pm рқ’„_{2}\sqrt{рқ’ҷ^{2}+1}$
    • Absorb the sign into the constant.
      • Let $рқ’„=\pm рқ’„_{2}$
    • Final general solution.
      • $рқ’ҡ=рқ’„\sqrt{рқ’ҷ^{2}+1}$
    • NOTE:
      • Since $рқ’ҷ^{2}+1>0$ for every real $рқ’ҷ$,
        • $\sqrt{рқ’ҷ^{2}+1}$ is always defined and positive.
      • As in the previous example,
        • the $\pm$ sign is absorbed into the arbitrary constant.

    • Core assumption:
      • The equation $\dfrac{рқ’…рқ’ҡ}{рқ’…рқ’•}=рқ’Ңрқ’ҡ$ can only be used when the problem states that the growth or decay rate is proportional to the current population.

        • the population may decrease in a way that is not proportional to $рқ’ҡ$.
      • In such a case,
        • the exponential model should not be assumed.
