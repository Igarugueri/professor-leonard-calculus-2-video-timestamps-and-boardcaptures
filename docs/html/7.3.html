  • [1:12]. Geometric interpretation of radicals.
    • Relate the radical to the Pythagorean Theorem and a right triangle.
    • Rewrite:
      • $\sqrt{1+рқ’ҷ^{2}}=\sqrt{1^{2}+рқ’ҷ^{2}}$
    • Determine whether the radical represents the hypotenuse or a leg.
      • General classification:
        • In a form like $\sqrt{рқ’Ӯ^{2}+рқ’ҷ^{2}}$, the radical is the hypotenuse.
        • In a form like $\sqrt{рқ’Ӯ^{2}-рқ’ҷ^{2}}$, the radical is a leg.
        • In a form like $\sqrt{рқ’ҷ^{2}-рқ’Ӯ^{2}}$, the radical is also a leg.
        • NOTE: The letter $рқ’Ӯ$ is just the constant in the expression. Its role depends on the form of the radical:
          • if the hypotenuse is the constant term, then $рқ’Ӯ$ is the hypotenuse;
          • if the variable $рқ’ҷ$ term is the hypotenuse, then $рқ’Ӯ$ is a leg.
      • Using the usual right-triangle notation:
        • Form $\sqrt{рқ’Ӯ^{2}+рқ“«^{2}}$: the radical is the hypotenuse $рқ“¬$.
        • Form $рқ’Ӯ=\sqrt{рқ“¬^{2}-рқ“«^{2}}$: the radical is a leg.
        • Form $рқ“«=\sqrt{рқ“¬^{2}-рқ’Ӯ^{2}}$: the radical is a leg.
  • [4:14]. Strategy for constructing the reference triangle.
    • Build the triangle for the model radical $\sqrt{1+рқ’ҷ^{2}}$.
      • adjacent side = $1$
      • opposite side = $рқ’ҷ$

  • [9:52]. Trigonometric integral obtained after substitution.
    • Use the identity:
      • $1+\tan^{2}(\theta)=\sec^{2}(\theta)$

      • $\displaystyle \int \sec^{3}(\theta)\,рқ’…\theta$
