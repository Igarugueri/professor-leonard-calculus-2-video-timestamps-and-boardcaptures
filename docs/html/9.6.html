1 - пјЎпҪӮпҪ“пҪҸпҪҢпҪ•пҪ”пҪ…гҖҖпјЈпҪҸпҪҺпҪ–пҪ…пҪ’пҪҮпҪ…пҪҺпҪғпҪ…

  • [0:03]. Introduction to Absolute Convergence.
    • Previous tests often required positive terms:
      • Integral Test:
        • positive,
        • continuous,
        • decreasing.
      • Direct Comparison Test:
        • positive terms.
      • Limit Comparison Test:
        • positive terms.
    • Main question:
      • how do we deal with series that have negative terms?
    • One case is already known:
      • if the signs alternate regularly,
        • use the Alternating Series Test.
    • But:
      • if the terms are sometimes positive and sometimes negative,
      • and they do not alternate regularly,
        • we need another idea.
  • [0:49]. Absolute convergence idea.
    • If the original series has negative terms,
      • take the absolute value of the terms.
    • This turns all terms into positive terms.
    • Then we can try tests that require positive terms:
      • Comparison Test.
      • Limit Comparison Test.
      • Integral Test.
      • Ratio Test.
      • Root Test.

1.1 - пјӨпҪ…пҪҶпҪүпҪҺпҪүпҪ”пҪүпҪҸпҪҺгҖҖпҪҸпҪҶгҖҖпјЎпҪӮпҪ“пҪҸпҪҢпҪ•пҪ”пҪ…гҖҖпјЈпҪҸпҪҺпҪ–пҪ…пҪ’пҪҮпҪ…пҪҺпҪғпҪ…

  • [1:31]. Formal definition of absolute convergence. вҖ“ [image]
    • Given a series:
      • $\displaystyle \sum рқ’Ӯ_{n}$
    • Take the absolute value of every term:
      • $\displaystyle \sum |рқ’Ӯ_{n}|$
    • iМІfМІ $\displaystyle \sum |рқ’Ӯ_{n}|$ converges,
      • tМІhМІeМІnМІ $\displaystyle \sum рқ’Ӯ_{n}$ is absolutely convergent.
  • [2:03]. Effect of absolute value.
    • The absolute value changes negative terms into positive terms.
    • Example:
      • $|рқ’Ӯ_{1}|+|рқ’Ӯ_{2}|+|рқ’Ӯ_{3}|+\cdots$
    • There are no negative terms left.
    • Therefore:
      • absolute convergence studies the convergence of the positive-size version of the series.
  • [4:25]. рҹ§© Example вҖ” Use for things like this: $\displaystyle \sum_{n=1}^{\infty}\dfrac{\sin(2рқ‘Ҙ)}{рқ’Ҹ^{2}}$
    • NOT (Alternating,noneincreasin and positive)
  • NМІOМІTМІEМІ вҖ” Meaning of absolute convergence.
    • Absolute convergence means:
      • the series still converges even after removing all sign cancellations.
    • This is stronger than ordinary convergence.
    • Reason:
      • if the positive-size version converges,
        • then the original signed series definitely converges.

  • [4:25]. рҹ§© Example вҖ” Absolute convergence: $\displaystyle \sum_{n=1}^{\infty}\dfrac{(-1)^{n-1}}{рқ’Ҹ^{2}}$

    • Then:

2 - пјЈпҪҸпҪҺпҪ„пҪүпҪ”пҪүпҪҸпҪҺпҪҒпҪҢгҖҖпјЈпҪҸпҪҺпҪ–пҪ…пҪ’пҪҮпҪ…пҪҺпҪғпҪ…

  • [image]
  • [7:19]. рҹ§© Example вҖ” Conditional convergence: $\displaystyle \sum_{n=1}^{\infty}\dfrac{(-1)^{n-1}}{рқ’Ҹ}$
    • This is the alternating harmonic series.
    • We already know:
      • it converges by the Alternating Series Test.
  • [8:24]. Check ordinary convergence.
    • The series is alternating.
    • Positive part:
      • $рқ’Ӯ_{n}=\dfrac{1}{рқ’Ҹ}$
    • Check the limit:
      • $\displaystyle \lim_{n\to\infty}\dfrac{1}{рқ’Ҹ}=0$
    • Check decreasing:
      • $\dfrac{1}{рқ’Ҹ+1}\le \dfrac{1}{рқ’Ҹ}$
    • Therefore, by the Alternating Series Test:
      • $\displaystyle \sum_{n=1}^{\infty}\dfrac{(-1)^{n-1}}{рқ’Ҹ}$ converges.
    • Important:
      • this proves ordinary convergence,
        • not absolute convergence.
  • [9:10]. Check absolute convergence.
    • Take the absolute value:
      • $\displaystyle \sum_{n=1}^{\infty}\left|\dfrac{(-1)^{n-1}}{рқ’Ҹ}\right|$
    • Since:
      • $|(-1)^{n-1}|=1$
    • Then:
      • $\displaystyle \sum_{n=1}^{\infty}\left|\dfrac{(-1)^{n-1}}{рқ’Ҹ}\right|=\sum_{n=1}^{\infty}\dfrac{1}{рқ’Ҹ}$
  • [10:13]. Harmonic Series.
    • The series:
      • $\displaystyle \sum_{n=1}^{\infty}\dfrac{1}{рқ’Ҹ}$
    • is the harmonic series.
    • The harmonic series diverges.
    • Therefore:
      • $\displaystyle \sum_{n=1}^{\infty}\left|\dfrac{(-1)^{n-1}}{рқ’Ҹ}\right|$ diverges.
  • [10:48]. Important conclusion.
    • The original alternating harmonic series converges:
      • $\displaystyle \sum_{n=1}^{\infty}\dfrac{(-1)^{n-1}}{рқ’Ҹ}$ converges.
    • But its absolute value series diverges:
      • $\displaystyle \sum_{n=1}^{\infty}\left|\dfrac{(-1)^{n-1}}{рқ’Ҹ}\right|$ diverges.
    • Therefore:
      • the original series is not absolutely convergent.
  • [12:10]. Definition of conditional convergence.
    • iМІfМІ the original series $\displaystyle \sum рқ’Ӯ_{n}$ converges, aМІnМІdМІ the absolute value series $\displaystyle \sum |рқ’Ӯ_{n}|$ diverges,
      • tМІhМІeМІnМІ the series $\displaystyle \sum рқ’Ӯ_{n}$ is conditionally convergent.
    • Symbolically:
      • $\displaystyle \sum рқ’Ӯ_{n}$ converges
      • but $\displaystyle \sum |рқ’Ӯ_{n}|$ diverges
    • Then:
      • $\displaystyle \sum рқ’Ӯ_{n}$ is conditionally convergent.
  • [13:49]. Conditional convergence for the alternating harmonic series.
    • Since:
      • $\displaystyle \sum_{n=1}^{\infty}\dfrac{(-1)^{n-1}}{рқ’Ҹ}$ converges,
    • But:
      • $\displaystyle \sum_{n=1}^{\infty}\left|\dfrac{(-1)^{n-1}}{рқ’Ҹ}\right|=\sum_{n=1}^{\infty}\dfrac{1}{рқ’Ҹ}$ diverges,
    • Then:
      • $\displaystyle \sum_{n=1}^{\infty}\dfrac{(-1)^{n-1}}{рқ’Ҹ}$ is conditionally convergent.
  • [15:15]. Absolute convergence is stronger than convergence.
    • Absolute convergence is stronger than ordinary convergence.
    • iМІfМІ a series is absolutely convergent,
      • tМІhМІeМІnМІ it is definitely convergent.
    • However:
      • a series can converge without being absolutely convergent.
    • That case is called:
      • conditional convergence.
  • NМІOМІTМІEМІ вҖ” Three possible outcomes.
    • Divergent:
      • the series does not converge.
    • Conditionally convergent:
      • $\displaystyle \sum рқ’Ӯ_{n}$ converges,
      • but $\displaystyle \sum |рқ’Ӯ_{n}|$ diverges.
    • Absolutely convergent:
      • $\displaystyle \sum |рқ’Ӯ_{n}|$ converges,
      • so $\displaystyle \sum рқ’Ӯ_{n}$ also converges.
    • Conceptual hierarchy:
      • absolute convergence is the strongest form of convergence.
      • conditional convergence is still convergence,
        • but it depends on sign cancellation.
      • divergence means there is no convergence.

3 -пјҙпҪҲпҪ…гҖҖпјІпҪҒпҪ”пҪүпҪҸгҖҖпјҙпҪ…пҪ“пҪ”

  • [image]
  • [25:56]. The Ratio Test.
    • The Ratio Test compares:
      • the next term $рқ’Ӯ_{n+1}$
      • with the current term $рқ’Ӯ_{n}$.
    • Main expression:
      • $\displaystyle \lim_{n\to\infty}\left|\dfrac{рқ’Ӯ_{n+1}}{рқ’Ӯ_{n}}\right|$
  • [26:24]. Structure of the Ratio Test.
    • Compute:
      • $рқ‘і=\displaystyle \lim_{n\to\infty}\left|\dfrac{рқ’Ӯ_{n+1}}{рқ’Ӯ_{n}}\right|$
    • Important:
      • $рқ’Ӯ_{n+1}$ goes on top.
      • $рқ’Ӯ_{n}$ goes on the bottom.
    • Be careful:
      • do not invert the ratio.
  • [28:15]. Ratio Test $\displaystyle \lim_{n\to\infty}\left|\dfrac{рқ’Ӯ_{n+1}}{рқ’Ӯ_{n}}\right|=рқ‘і$ outcomes.
    • iМІfМІ $рқ‘і<1$
      • tМІhМІeМІnМІ the series is absolutely convergent.
    • iМІfМІ $рқ‘і>1$
      • tМІhМІeМІnМІ the series diverges.
    • iМІfМІ $рқ‘і=1$
      • tМІhМІeМІnМІ the test is inconclusive.
  • NМІOМІTМІEМІ вҖ” Why the Ratio Test is powerful.
    • The Ratio Test includes absolute values.
    • Therefore:
      • it can prove absolute convergence.
    • It is especially useful for:
      • factorials.
      • powers involving $рқ’Ҹ$.
      • expressions where $\dfrac{рқ’Ӯ_{n+1}}{рқ’Ӯ_{n}}$ simplifies nicely.
  • NМІOМІTМІEМІ вҖ” Ratio Test versus Divergence Test.
    • Usually, we first check the Divergence Test.
    • But with factorials or complicated powers,
      • the Divergence Test may be awkward.
    • The Ratio Test can often detect:
      • absolute convergence,
      • or divergence,
      • without needing a separate Divergence Test first.
    • If the Ratio Test gives $рқ‘і=1$,
      • then we must try another test.

      • $0/0$

      • $рқ’Ҹ=рқ’Ҹ^{1}$
      • $рқ’Ҹ^{1}=рқ’Ҹ^{n/n}$
      • $рқ’Ҹ^{n/n}=(рқ’Ҹ^{1/n})^{n}$
    • Therefore:
      • $\dfrac{рқ’Ҹ}{2^{n}}=\dfrac{(рқ’Ҹ^{1/n})^{n}}{2^{n}}$
      • $=\left(\dfrac{рқ’Ҹ^{1/n}}{2}\right)^{n}$
    • Apply the Root Test:
      • $рқ‘і=\displaystyle \lim_{n\to\infty}\sqrt[n]{\left|\dfrac{рқ’Ҹ}{2^{n}}\right|}$
      • $=\displaystyle \lim_{n\to\infty}\sqrt[n]{\left(\dfrac{рқ’Ҹ^{1/n}}{2}\right)^{n}}$
      • $=\displaystyle \lim_{n\to\infty}\dfrac{рқ’Ҹ^{1/n}}{2}$
    • Since:
      • $\displaystyle \lim_{n\to\infty}рқ’Ҹ^{1/n}=1$
    • Then:
      • $рқ‘і=\dfrac{1}{2}$
    • Since:
      • $рқ‘і=\dfrac{1}{2}<1$
    • Therefore:
      • $\displaystyle \sum_{n=1}^{\infty}\dfrac{рқ’Ҹ}{2^{n}}$ converges by the Root Test.
  • [1:45:12]. вқҫ Strategy for trigonometric functions.
    • If the series contains:
      • $\sin(рқ’Ҹ)$,
      • $\cos(рқ’Ҹ)$,
      • $\sin(2рқ’Ҹ)$,
      • or similar oscillating terms,
    • Then:
      • it may not be positive,
      • and may not alternate regularly.
    • вӯҗ Model Example:
      • $\displaystyle \sum_{n=1}^{\infty}\dfrac{\sin(рқ’Ҹ)}{\sqrt{рқ’Ҹ^{3}+1}}$
    • In that case:
      • try absolute value.
    • Take:
      • $\left|\dfrac{\sin(рқ’Ҹ)}{\sqrt{рқ’Ҹ^{3}+1}}\right|$
      • $=\dfrac{|\sin(рқ’Ҹ)|}{\sqrt{рқ’Ҹ^{3}+1}}$
    • Since:
      • $|\sin(рқ’Ҹ)|\le 1$
    • Then:
      • $\dfrac{|\sin(рқ’Ҹ)|}{\sqrt{рқ’Ҹ^{3}+1}}\le \dfrac{1}{\sqrt{рқ’Ҹ^{3}+1}}$
    • End behavior:
      • $\dfrac{1}{\sqrt{рқ’Ҹ^{3}+1}}$ behaves like $\dfrac{1}{рқ’Ҹ^{3/2}}$
    • Since:
      • $\displaystyle \sum_{n=1}^{\infty}\dfrac{1}{рқ’Ҹ^{3/2}}$ converges
    • Then:
      • use absolute value plus comparison.
    • Therefore:
      • the original series is absolutely convergent.
  • NМІOМІTМІEМІ вҖ” Final strategy.
    • Do not randomly choose a test.
    • First identify the structure:
      • nonzero term limit вҮ’ Divergence Test.
      • geometric form вҮ’ Geometric Series Test.
      • telescoping cancellation вҮ’ Telescoping Series.
      • $\dfrac{1}{рқ’Ҹ^{рқ’‘}}$ form вҮ’ рқ’‘-Series.
      • positive continuous decreasing function вҮ’ Integral Test.
      • positive terms like a known series вҮ’ Comparison.
      • alternating signs вҮ’ Alternating Series Test.
      • factorials вҮ’ Ratio Test.
      • $рқ’Ҹ$-th powers вҮ’ Root Test.
      • trig oscillation вҮ’ absolute value plus comparison.
