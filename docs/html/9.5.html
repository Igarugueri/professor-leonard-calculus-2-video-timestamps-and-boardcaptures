    • Given series:
      • $\displaystyle \sum_{n=1}^{\infty}\dfrac{(-1)^{n-1}}{рқ’Ҹ}$
    • Expand the first terms:
      • $1-\dfrac{1}{2}+\dfrac{1}{3}-\dfrac{1}{4}+\dfrac{1}{5}-\cdots$
    • Key observation:
      • the signs alternate:
        • $+,-,+,-,+,\ldots$
      • therefore, this is an alternating series.
  • Identifying the alternating factor.
    • The factor that creates the sign change is:
      • $(-1)^{n-1}$
    • If the series starts with a positive term,
      • we often use $(-1)^{n-1}$.
    • If the series starts with a negative term,
      • we often use $(-1)^{n}$.
    • Key idea:
      • the $(-1)$ factor controls the signs.
      • the rest of the expression gives the positive size of each term.
  • [3:20]. General structure of an alternating series.
    • Any alternating series can usually be written as:
      • $\displaystyle \sum_{n=1}^{\infty}(-1)^{n-1}\cdot рқ’Ӯ_{n}$
    • Here:
      • $(-1)^{n-1}$ creates the alternating signs.
      • $рқ’Ӯ_{n}$ represents the positive part of the terms.
    • In this example:
      • $рқ’Ӯ_{n}=\dfrac{1}{рқ’Ҹ}$
    • Therefore:
      • $\displaystyle \sum_{n=1}^{\infty}\dfrac{(-1)^{n-1}}{рқ’Ҹ}=\sum_{n=1}^{\infty}(-1)^{n-1}\cdot \dfrac{1}{рқ’Ҹ}$
    • Important:
      • $рқ’Ӯ_{n}$ itself is positive.
      • the alternating sign is not considered part of $рқ’Ӯ_{n}$.
  • [4:17]. Harmonic Series versus Alternating Harmonic Series.
    • Harmonic Series:
      • $\displaystyle \sum_{n=1}^{\infty}\dfrac{1}{рқ’Ҹ}$
      • $=1+\dfrac{1}{2}+\dfrac{1}{3}+\dfrac{1}{4}+\cdots$
      • diverges.
    • Alternating Harmonic Series:
      • $\displaystyle \sum_{n=1}^{\infty}\dfrac{(-1)^{n-1}}{рқ’Ҹ}$
      • $=1-\dfrac{1}{2}+\dfrac{1}{3}-\dfrac{1}{4}+\cdots$
      • converges.
    • Important:
      • adding alternating signs can change the convergence behavior.
      • the harmonic series diverges,
        • but the alternating harmonic series converges.

  • [7:53]. The Alternating Series Test.
    • The Alternating Series Test applies to series of the form:
      • $\displaystyle \sum_{n=1}^{\infty}(-1)^{n-1}\cdot рқ’Ӯ_{n}$
      • or $\displaystyle \sum_{n=1}^{\infty}(-1)^{n}\cdot рқ’Ӯ_{n}$
    • Where:
      • $рқ’Ӯ_{n}>0$
    • The test checks two things:
      • the positive terms $рқ’Ӯ_{n}$ must be decreasing.
      • the positive terms $рқ’Ӯ_{n}$ must approach $0$.
  • [9:24]. Condition 1 вҖ” Decreasing sequence.
    • The positive sequence must be decreasing:
      • $рқ’Ӯ_{n+1}\le рқ’Ӯ_{n}$
    • Meaning:
      • each next positive term is less than or equal to the previous one.
    • This is important because:
      • the alternating partial sums move back and forth,
      • but the jumps get smaller and smaller.
  • [10:30]. Condition 2 вҖ” Limit of the positive terms.
    • The positive terms must approach $0$:
      • $\displaystyle \lim_{n\to\infty}рқ’Ӯ_{n}=0$
    • This is like the Divergence Test,
      • but applied to the positive part $рқ’Ӯ_{n}$.
    • Important:
      • do not include $(-1)^{n}$ or $(-1)^{n-1}$ when checking this limit.
      • only use the positive part $рқ’Ӯ_{n}$.
  • [11:26]. Conclusion of the Alternating Series Test.
    • iМІfМІ $рқ’Ӯ_{n+1}\le рқ’Ӯ_{n}$ aМІnМІdМІ $\displaystyle \lim_{n\to\infty}рқ’Ӯ_{n}=0$
      • tМІhМІeМІnМІ the alternating series $\displaystyle \sum (-1)^{n-1}рқ’Ӯ_{n}$ converges.
    • In words:
      • if the positive terms decrease to $0$,
        • then the alternating series converges.
  • [11:26]. Recap вҖ” How to use the Alternating Series Test.
    • First, verify that the series is alternating.
      • the signs must alternate:
        • $+,-,+,-,\ldots$
        • or $-,+,-,+,\ldots$
      • this usually comes from:
        • $(-1)^{n}$
        • or $(-1)^{n-1}$
    • Then, separate the series into:

      • or $\displaystyle \sum (-1)^{n}рқ’Ӯ_{n}$
    • Important:
      • $рқ’Ӯ_{n}$ is only the positive part.
      • do not include $(-1)^{n}$ or $(-1)^{n-1}$ inside $рқ’Ӯ_{n}$.
    • Step 1 вҖ” Check the limit of the positive terms:
      • $\displaystyle \lim_{n\to\infty}рқ’Ӯ_{n}=0$

        • tМІhМІeМІnМІ the alternating series converges.
    • Key idea:
      • the test is not applied to the whole signed term.
      • it is applied to the positive sequence $рқ’Ӯ_{n}$.
  • [13:58]. Graphical and conceptual justification.
    • Imagine the partial sums moving back and forth:
      • add a positive term,
      • subtract a smaller term,
      • add an even smaller term,
      • subtract an even smaller term,
      • and so on.
    • Because the terms are decreasing:
      • each jump is smaller than the previous jump.
    • Because the terms approach $0$:
      • the jumps eventually become tiny.
    • Therefore:
      • the partial sums settle toward one finite value.
      • this is why the alternating series converges.
  • [13:58]. Ping-pong model.
    • Think of a ping-pong rally:
      • the ball moves from one side to the other.
      • each hit travels a shorter distance.
      • eventually, the movement gets closer and closer to a central point.
    • Alternating series behave similarly:
      • the partial sums jump above and below the final sum.
      • the jumps become smaller.
      • the sequence of partial sums converges.

    • Therefore:

        • but each bounce is smaller.
      • this is why the alternating series converges.

  • [45:28]. Finding error in alternating series.
    • If a series $\displaystyle \sum рқ’Ӯ_{n}$ converges,
      • then it has some sum $рқ‘ә$.
    • The sequence of partial sums satisfies:
      • $\displaystyle \lim_{n\to\infty}рқ‘ә_{n}=рқ‘ә$
    • Therefore:
      • $\displaystyle \lim_{n\to\infty}(рқ‘ә-рқ‘ә_{n})=0$
    • Meaning:
      • if $рқ’Ҹ$ actually goes to infinity,
        • the partial sums approach the exact sum.
      • but if we stop at a finite $рқ’Ҹ$,
        • there will be some error between $рқ‘ә$ and $рқ‘ә_{n}$.
  • [50:00]. Defining the error.
    • The error is called:
      • $рқ‘№_{n}$
    • Definition:
      • $рқ‘№_{n}=рқ‘ә-рқ‘ә_{n}$
    • Meaning:
      • error = whole sum - partial sum
    • In words:
      • $рқ‘ә$ is the exact infinite sum.
      • $рқ‘ә_{n}$ is the partial sum after $рқ’Ҹ$ terms.
      • $рқ‘№_{n}$ is the difference between them.
  • [53:36]. Alternating Series Estimation Theorem.
    • For alternating series only:
      • $|рқ‘№_{n}|=|рқ‘ә-рқ‘ә_{n}|\le рқ’Ӯ_{n+1}$
    • Meaning:
      • the absolute error is less than or equal to the first omitted term.
    • If we stop at $рқ‘ә_{n}$,
      • the next positive term is $рқ’Ӯ_{n+1}$.
    • Therefore:
      • the error is no larger than $рқ’Ӯ_{n+1}$.
  • [55:04]. Why the first omitted term controls the error.
    • Suppose the exact sum is:
      • $рқ‘ә=рқ’Ӯ_{1}-рқ’Ӯ_{2}+рқ’Ӯ_{3}-\cdots-рқ’Ӯ_{n}+рқ’Ӯ_{n+1}-рқ’Ӯ_{n+2}+\cdots$
    • The partial sum is:
      • $рқ‘ә_{n}=рқ’Ӯ_{1}-рқ’Ӯ_{2}+рқ’Ӯ_{3}-\cdots-рқ’Ӯ_{n}$
    • Therefore, the remaining tail is:
      • $рқ‘ә-рқ‘ә_{n}=рқ’Ӯ_{n+1}-рқ’Ӯ_{n+2}+рқ’Ӯ_{n+3}-\cdots$
    • Since the positive terms are decreasing:
      • $рқ’Ӯ_{n+2}\le рқ’Ӯ_{n+1}$
      • $рқ’Ӯ_{n+3}\le рқ’Ӯ_{n+2}$
      • and so on.
    • Therefore:
      • The remaining terms alternate signs and partially cancel each other,
      • and since their sizes decrease, the total remaining error cannot exceed the first omitted term.
    • So:
