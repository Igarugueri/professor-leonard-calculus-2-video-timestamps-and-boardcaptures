        • $4\displaystyle \int \sec^{5} рқ’ҷ\,рқ’…рқ’ҷ=\sec^{3} рқ’ҷ\tan рқ’ҷ+3\displaystyle \int \sec^{3} рқ’ҷ\,рқ’…рқ’ҷ$
      • Substitute the previous result for $\displaystyle \int \sec^{3} рқ’ҷ\,рқ’…рқ’ҷ$:
        • $4\displaystyle \int \sec^{5} рқ’ҷ\,рқ’…рқ’ҷ=\sec^{3} рқ’ҷ\tan рқ’ҷ+\left(\dfrac{3}{2}\right)\sec рқ’ҷ\tan рқ’ҷ+\left(\dfrac{3}{2}\right)\ln|\sec рқ’ҷ+\tan рқ’ҷ|$
      • Final result:
        • $\displaystyle \int \sec^{5} рқ’ҷ\,рқ’…рқ’ҷ=\left(\dfrac{1}{4}\right)\sec^{3} рқ’ҷ\tan рқ’ҷ+\left(\dfrac{3}{8}\right)\sec рқ’ҷ\tan рқ’ҷ+\left(\dfrac{3}{8}\right)\ln|\sec рқ’ҷ+\tan рқ’ҷ|+рқ“’$
    • Key idea
      • Odd powers of secant are handled by peeling off a factor $\sec^{2} рқ’ҷ$ and using Integration by Parts.
      • The identity $\tan^{2} рқ’ҷ=\sec^{2} рқ’ҷ-1$ converts the remaining expression back into secant powers, creating a recurrence relation.

  • [1:29:40]. Development and proof of the Reduction Formula. вҖ“ [рҹ“·image-1] вҖ“ [рҹ“·image-2]
    • NOTE: It is not only for sine. This is specifically the reduction formula for вҲ« sinвҒҝx dx, but similar reduction formulas also exist for cosine, secant, and other families of integrals.
    • Goal:
      • Reduce $\displaystyle \int \sin^{рқ’Ҹ} рқ’ҷ\,рқ’…рқ’ҷ$ to an integral involving $\sin^{рқ’Ҹ-2} рқ’ҷ$.
      • Assume $рқ’Ҹ\ge 2$.
    • Split the integrand:
      • $\displaystyle \int \sin^{рқ’Ҹ} рқ’ҷ\,рқ’…рқ’ҷ=\displaystyle \int \sin^{рқ’Ҹ-1} рқ’ҷ\cdot \sin рқ’ҷ\,рқ’…рқ’ҷ$
    • Apply Integration by Parts.
      • Choose:
        • $рқ’–=\sin^{рқ’Ҹ-1} рқ’ҷ$
        • $рқ’…\mathcal{V}=\sin рқ’ҷ\,рқ’…рқ’ҷ$
      • Then:
        • $рқ’…рқ’–=(рқ’Ҹ-1)\sin^{рқ’Ҹ-2} рқ’ҷ\cos рқ’ҷ\,рқ’…рқ’ҷ$
        • $\mathcal{V}=-\cos рқ’ҷ$
    • Substitute into the formula.
      • $\displaystyle \int \sin^{рқ’Ҹ} рқ’ҷ\,рқ’…рқ’ҷ$
        • $=рқ’–\mathcal{V}-\displaystyle \int \mathcal{V}\,рқ’…рқ’–$
        • $=-\sin^{рқ’Ҹ-1} рқ’ҷ\cos рқ’ҷ+(рқ’Ҹ-1)\displaystyle \int \cos^{2} рқ’ҷ\sin^{рқ’Ҹ-2} рқ’ҷ\,рқ’…рқ’ҷ$
    • Rewrite $\cos^{2} рқ’ҷ$ in terms of sine.
      • Use:
        • $\cos^{2} рқ’ҷ=1-\sin^{2} рқ’ҷ$
      • Then:
        • $\displaystyle \int \sin^{рқ’Ҹ} рқ’ҷ\,рқ’…рқ’ҷ$
        • $=-\sin^{рқ’Ҹ-1} рқ’ҷ\cos рқ’ҷ+(рқ’Ҹ-1)\displaystyle \int (1-\sin^{2} рқ’ҷ)\sin^{рқ’Ҹ-2} рқ’ҷ\,рқ’…рқ’ҷ$
    • Expand the integral.
      • $=-\sin^{рқ’Ҹ-1} рқ’ҷ\cos рқ’ҷ+(рқ’Ҹ-1)\displaystyle \int \sin^{рқ’Ҹ-2} рқ’ҷ\,рқ’…рқ’ҷ-(рқ’Ҹ-1)\displaystyle \int \sin^{рқ’Ҹ} рқ’ҷ\,рқ’…рқ’ҷ$
    • Collect like terms.
      • Add $(рқ’Ҹ-1)\displaystyle \int \sin^{рқ’Ҹ} рқ’ҷ\,рқ’…рқ’ҷ$ to both sides:
        • $рқ’Ҹ\displaystyle \int \sin^{рқ’Ҹ} рқ’ҷ\,рқ’…рқ’ҷ=-\sin^{рқ’Ҹ-1} рқ’ҷ\cos рқ’ҷ+(рқ’Ҹ-1)\displaystyle \int \sin^{рқ’Ҹ-2} рқ’ҷ\,рқ’…рқ’ҷ$
    • Divide by $рқ’Ҹ$.
      • $\displaystyle \int \sin^{рқ’Ҹ} рқ’ҷ\,рқ’…рқ’ҷ=-\left(\dfrac{1}{рқ’Ҹ}\right)\sin^{рқ’Ҹ-1} рқ’ҷ\cos рқ’ҷ+\left(\dfrac{рқ’Ҹ-1}{рқ’Ҹ}\right)\displaystyle \int \sin^{рқ’Ҹ-2} рқ’ҷ\,рқ’…рқ’ҷ$
    • Final Reduction Formula for sine:
      • $\displaystyle \int \sin^{рқ’Ҹ} рқ’ҷ\,рқ’…рқ’ҷ=-\left(\dfrac{1}{рқ’Ҹ}\right)\sin^{рқ’Ҹ-1} рқ’ҷ\cos рқ’ҷ+\left(\dfrac{рқ’Ҹ-1}{рқ’Ҹ}\right)\displaystyle \int \sin^{рқ’Ҹ-2} рқ’ҷ\,рқ’…рқ’ҷ$
