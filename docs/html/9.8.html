  • [1:40]. Requirement of differentiability.
    • If $рқ’Ү$ has a power series representation,
      • then all the way to the $n$-th derivative of $рқ’Ү$ must exist at $рқ‘Ә$.
    • Symbolically:
      • $рқ’Ү^{(n)}(рқ‘Ә)$ exists.
    • Reason:
      • a power series contains powers of $(рқ’ҷ-рқ‘Ә)$.
    • Since powers can be differentiated repeatedly,
      • the function represented by the power series must also allow repeated differentiation.
    • Key idea:
      • if the series has terms up to power $n$,
      • then taking derivatives up to order $n$ makes sense.
    • Therefore:
      • Taylor series are deeply connected to derivatives.

      • they are determined by the values of $рқ’Ү$ and its derivatives at the center $рқ‘Ә$.
  • [5:35]. Start from the expanded power series.
    • Write:
      • $рқ’Ү(рқ’ҷ)=рқ’Ӯ_{0}+рқ’Ӯ_{1}(рқ’ҷ-рқ‘Ә)+рқ’Ӯ_{2}(рқ’ҷ-рқ‘Ә)^{2}+рқ’Ӯ_{3}(рқ’ҷ-рқ‘Ә)^{3}+\cdots+рқ’Ӯ_{n}(рқ’ҷ-рқ‘Ә)^{n}+\cdots$
    • This allows us to see:
      • what happens to each term when we differentiate.
    • The key is to track:
      • which term becomes a constant after enough derivatives.
  • [7:40]. Take derivatives term by term.
    • First derivative:
      • $рқ’Ү'(рқ’ҷ)=рқ’Ӯ_{1}+2рқ’Ӯ_{2}(рқ’ҷ-рқ‘Ә)+3рқ’Ӯ_{3}(рқ’ҷ-рқ‘Ә)^{2}+\cdots+nрқ’Ӯ_{n}(рқ’ҷ-рқ‘Ә)^{n-1}+\cdots$
    • Second derivative:
      • $рқ’Ү''(рқ’ҷ)=2\cdot 1\cdot рқ’Ӯ_{2}+3\cdot 2\cdot рқ’Ӯ_{3}(рқ’ҷ-рқ‘Ә)+\cdots+n(n-1)рқ’Ӯ_{n}(рқ’ҷ-рқ‘Ә)^{n-2}+\cdots$
    • Third derivative:
      • $рқ’Ү'''(рқ’ҷ)=3\cdot 2\cdot 1\cdot рқ’Ӯ_{3}+\cdots+n(n-1)(n-2)рқ’Ӯ_{n}(рқ’ҷ-рқ‘Ә)^{n-3}+\cdots$
    • Pattern:
      • every derivative lowers the power by $1$.
      • every derivative brings down one more factor.
    • Therefore:
      • the $n$-th derivative eventually brings down all factors from $n$ to $1$.
  • [12:19]. Factorial pattern.
    • Repeated differentiation produces:
      • $n(n-1)(n-2)\cdots 3\cdot 2\cdot 1$
    • This is:
      • $n!$
    • Meaning:
      • factorials appear naturally because derivatives keep bringing down powers.
    • Example idea:
      • differentiating $(рқ’ҷ-рқ‘Ә)^{n}$ once gives $n(рқ’ҷ-рқ‘Ә)^{n-1}$.
      • differentiating again gives $n(n-1)(рқ’ҷ-рқ‘Ә)^{n-2}$.
      • after $n$ derivatives, the power becomes $0:(рқ’ҷ-рқ‘Ә)^{n-n}$
    • Since:
      • $(рқ’ҷ-рқ‘Ә)^{0}=1$
    • The remaining coefficient is:
      • $n!рқ’Ӯ_{n}$
  • [13:50]. What happens to the $n$-th term.
    • Start with the $n$-th term:
      • $рқ’Ӯ_{n}(рқ’ҷ-рқ‘Ә)^{n}$
    • After one derivative:
      • $nрқ’Ӯ_{n}(рқ’ҷ-рқ‘Ә)^{n-1}$
    • After two derivatives:
      • $n(n-1)рқ’Ӯ_{n}(рқ’ҷ-рқ‘Ә)^{n-2}$
    • After three derivatives:
      • $n(n-1)(n-2)рқ’Ӯ_{n}(рқ’ҷ-рқ‘Ә)^{n-3}$
    • After $n$ derivatives:
      • $n!рқ’Ӯ_{n}$
    • Important:
      • the $n$-th term becomes a constant after exactly $n$ derivatives.
  • [16:50]. Evaluate at the center $рқ‘Ә$.
    • When $рқ’ҷ=рқ‘Ә$:
      • every term containing $(рқ’ҷ-рқ‘Ә)$ becomes $0$.
    • Reason:
      • $рқ‘Ә-рқ‘Ә=0$
    • Therefore:
      • all terms that still contain a positive power of $(рқ’ҷ-рқ‘Ә)$ vanish.
    • The only surviving term is:
      • $n!рқ’Ӯ_{n}$
    • This is the key trick:
      • differentiating enough times isolates $рқ’Ӯ_{n}$.
    • Therefore:
      • $рқ’Ү^{(n)}(рқ‘Ә)=n!рқ’Ӯ_{n}$
  • [18:17]. Solve for the coefficient.
    • From:
      • $рқ’Ү^{(n)}(рқ‘Ә)=n!рқ’Ӯ_{n}$
    • Divide by $n!$:
      • $рқ’Ӯ_{n}=\dfrac{рқ’Ү^{(n)}(рқ‘Ә)}{n!}$
    • This is the central result:
      • every coefficient $рқ’Ӯ_{n}$ is determined by the $n$-th derivative of $рқ’Ү$ at the center.
    • Meaning:
      • the derivatives of $рқ’Ү$ at $рқ‘Ә$ encode the whole power series.

    • Substitute:

    • Into:

    • Taylor series:
      • $рқ’Ү(рқ’ҷ)=\displaystyle \sum_{n=0}^{\infty}\left[\dfrac{рқ’Ү^{(n)}(рқ‘Ә)}{n!}\right](рқ’ҷ-рқ‘Ә)^{n}$
    • Meaning:
      • Taylor series rebuilds $рқ’Ү$ from its derivatives at $рқ‘Ә$.
    • Each term uses:
      • one derivative value,
      • one factorial,
      • one power of $(рқ’ҷ-рқ‘Ә)$.
  • [21:43]. Expanded Taylor series.

      • $+\cdots$
    • Interpretation:
      • the first term gives the value of the function at the center.
      • the second term uses the slope at the center.
      • the third term uses curvature information.
      • higher terms add more local information about the function.
    • Big idea:
      • Taylor series turns derivative information at one point into a full function representation.

  • [24:00]. Maclaurin series.
    • A Maclaurin series is:
      • a Taylor series centered at $рқ‘Ә=0$.
    • In other words:
      • Maclaurin is not a different kind of series.
      • it is a special case of the Taylor series.
    • The only change is:
      • the center $рқ‘Ә$ becomes $0$.
  • [24:38]. Taylor versus Maclaurin.
    • Every Maclaurin series is a Taylor series.
    • But not every Taylor series is a Maclaurin series.
    • Reason:
      • Maclaurin is the special case $рқ‘Ә=0$.
    • Analogy:
      • every square is a rectangle,
      • but not every rectangle is a square.
    • Similarly:
      • every Maclaurin series is a Taylor series,
      • but only Taylor series centered at $0$ are Maclaurin series.
  • [25:03]. What changes when $рқ‘Ә=0$.
    • Taylor series:
      • $рқ’Ү(рқ’ҷ)=\displaystyle \sum_{n=0}^{\infty}\left[\dfrac{рқ’Ү^{(n)}(рқ‘Ә)}{n!}\right](рқ’ҷ-рқ‘Ә)^{n}$
    • If:
      • $рқ‘Ә=0$
    • Then:
      • $(рқ’ҷ-рқ‘Ә)^{n}=(рқ’ҷ-0)^{n}$
      • $(рқ’ҷ-0)^{n}=рқ’ҷ^{n}$
    • Therefore:
      • the formula becomes simpler.
  • [25:42]. Maclaurin formula.
    • Since:
      • $рқ‘Ә=0$
    • Then:
      • $рқ’Ү(рқ’ҷ)=\displaystyle \sum_{n=0}^{\infty}\left[\dfrac{рқ’Ү^{(n)}(0)}{n!}\right]рқ’ҷ^{n}$
    • Expanded form:
      • $рқ’Ү(рқ’ҷ)=рқ’Ү(0)$
      • $+\left[\dfrac{рқ’Ү'(0)}{1!}\right]рқ’ҷ$
      • $+\left[\dfrac{рқ’Ү''(0)}{2!}\right]рқ’ҷ^{2}$
      • $+\left[\dfrac{рқ’Ү'''(0)}{3!}\right]рқ’ҷ^{3}$
      • $+\cdots$
    • Meaning:
      • all derivatives are evaluated at $0$.
      • all powers are powers of $рқ’ҷ$.
      • there is no $(рқ’ҷ-рқ‘Ә)$, because the center is $0$.
  • NМІOМІTМІEМІ вҖ” Main difference.
    • Taylor series:
      • centered at any value $рқ‘Ә$.
    • Maclaurin series:
      • centered only at $0$.
    • So:
      • Taylor uses $(рқ’ҷ-рқ‘Ә)^{n}$.
      • Maclaurin uses $рқ’ҷ^{n}$.
